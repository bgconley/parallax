# Microcopy, Voice, and Tone

## 1. Voice thesis

The app should sound calm, precise, and helpful. It should not sound like a coach, therapist, manager, gamified mascot, or motivational poster.

## 2. Tone principles

- Plain language.
- Short sentences.
- No shame.
- No infantilizing language.
- No false certainty from AI.
- No clinical claims.
- Useful uncertainty.
- Lead with the action.

## 3. Good copy patterns

Capture:

- “Type anything. I’ll help shape it.”
- “Saved. I’ll refine this when you’re online.”
- “Want this as a reminder, a tiny start, or a plan?”

Today:

- “This looks like a full day.”
- “Three things fit comfortably.”
- “Want a lighter version?”

Now:

- “Put the dishes next to the sink.”
- “Done enough: the counter is clear enough to cook.”
- “Need a smaller step?”

Stuck:

- “What is making this hard?”
- “Let’s make the next step smaller.”
- “This can wait. Want me to make a restart plan?”

Timing:

- “Last similar session: 18 min.”
- “This estimate is still learning.”
- “Was this session normal?”

Privacy:

- “Your raw notes stay in your account.”
- “AI processing helps create plans and estimates.”
- “You can export or delete your data from Settings.”

## 4. Avoid

- “Crush your goals.”
- “You failed.”
- “No excuses.”
- “Our AI knows exactly...”
- “Clinically proven to treat ADHD.”
- “Optimize your life.”
- “Productivity score.”

## 5. AI uncertainty language

Use:

- “This looks like...”
- “Based on your past sessions...”
- “Low confidence.”
- “Want to correct this?”

Avoid:

- “Definitely.”
- “Guaranteed.”
- “You always...”
- “You should...”

## 6. Error and degraded state copy

Network unavailable:

“Saved on this iPhone. I’ll sync and refine it when you’re back online.”

Model unavailable:

“I can save this now. AI planning is temporarily unavailable.”

Sync conflict:

“This changed on another device. Pick the version you want to keep.”

Permission denied:

“No problem. You can still use the app without this permission.”
