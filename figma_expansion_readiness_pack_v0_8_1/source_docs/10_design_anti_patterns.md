# UI/UX Anti-Patterns to Avoid

## 1. The cockpit problem

Do not make the home screen a command center. It should be an entryway, not an aircraft panel.

## 2. The Jira-lite problem

Do not make task detail look like an issue tracker. Avoid status taxonomies, custom field grids, and ticket-like metadata fields in the default UI.

## 3. The AI-chat-first problem

Do not make the assistant the app. AI should structure, suggest, explain, and repair. It should not force every action through conversational turn-taking.

## 4. The setup-tax problem

Do not ask users to configure their productivity philosophy before they can capture a thought.

## 5. The shame dashboard problem

Do not lead with overdue counts, failure metrics, streaks, or productivity scores.

## 6. The over-reminder problem

Do not silently add reminders or nag aggressively. Reminders should be chosen and personalizable.

## 7. The everything-is-a-project problem

Some tasks need only capture. Some need a reminder. Some need a timer. Some need a full plan. Do not route everything into a full project workflow.

## 8. The invisible-power problem

Do not hide critical primary actions behind gestures or menus. Swipes and context menus are shortcuts, not required paths.

## 9. The decorative-AI problem

Do not show sparkle icons, magic language, or model explanations everywhere. Show AI only when it helps the user act, correct, or trust the result.

## 10. The sterile-minimalism problem

Do not confuse calm with empty. The app should feel habitable and warm, not blank and cold.
